4.1(a) Construct sample spaces of random experiments; identify and specify events, and perform set operations on events.
 

  
 
4.2(b) Construct sample spaces of random experiments; identify and specify events, and perform set operations on events.
 

  
 
4.3(c) Construct simple Probability measures for discrete and random sample spaces; e.g., uniform distributions over sample spaces.
 

  
 
4.4(d) Compute probabilities by counting; Learn to be able to count permutations and combinations of n objects.
 

  
 
4.5(e) Compute conditional probability of events, and determine dependence/independence of events.
 

  
 
4.6(f) Use Baye's law to compute &quot;a posteriori&quot; probabilities of events.
 

  
 
4.7(g) Use probability models to calculate (I) the odds of winning/losing in card and other games that involve random experiments, (ii) errors in binary communication channels, and (iii) Bayesian inference of symbols sent over noisy communication channels.
 

  
 
4.8(h) Obtain random variables corresponding to random experiments; Specify probability density and cumulative distribution functions for both discrete and continuous random variables. Calculate the distributions for functions of random variables.
 

  
 
4.9(i) Compute the expected value, variance, and higher-order moments of random variables (for both discrete and continuous types).
 

  
 
4.10(j) Use standard discrete random variables used in science and engineering, including Bernoulli, binomial, multinomial, geometric, and Poisson random variables. Learn elementary applications to traffic modeling.
 

  
 
4.11(k) Use standard continuous random variables used in science and engineering, including , exponential, Gaussian, Cauchy, and Gamma, distributions. Learn elementary applications to noise modeling.
 

  
 
4.12(l) Use Markov and Chebyshev inequalities to obtain bounds on probability of events.
 

  
 
4.13(m) Use characteristic functions of random variables to compute (I) distributions of sums of independent random variables, and (ii) moments of random variables.
 

  
 
4.14(n) Usel 2-dimensional random vectors to model experiments with two simultaneous outcomes. Compute the distributions of functions of 2-d random variables. Compute marginal and conditional distributions of random variables.
 

  
 
4.15(o) Use law of large numbers to determine convergence of sample estimates to true parameters.
 

  
 
4.16(p) Use the central limit theorem to compute probabilities.
 

  
 
4.17(q) Use of chi-square test to determine the goodness of the fit of a distribution to data.
 

  
 
4.18(r) Use deterministic algorithms (that can be implemented on computers) to generate pseudo-random numbers.
 

  
 
4.19(s) Several homework assignments delving on core concepts and reinforcing analytical skills learned in class. Opportunity to conduct a matlab-based design project requiring some independent reading, programming, simulations and technical writing.
 

  
 
4.20(t) Opportunities to interact weekly with the instructor and the teaching assistant(s) during regular office hours and discussion sections in order to further the students' learning experience and the students' interest in the material.
